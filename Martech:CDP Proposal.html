The Problem
KPay is spending on growth without being able to measure it.
Every month, marketing campaigns run, leads are handed to BD, and merchants onboard through the DMO — yet Digital Growth has no reliable way to connect any of that spend to a merchant activation or a dollar of GPV. Attribution breaks the moment a lead leaves the form. The BD pipeline is invisible. The DMO funnel is a black box. By the time a merchant reaches their first transaction, the campaign that brought them in is already forgotten.
The result: budget decisions are made on guesswork, BD conversations start without context, and Digital Growth has no credible story to tell Finance. KPay cannot prove what is working, cannot fix what is not, and cannot scale what it cannot see.
Today
Marketing spend cannot be tied to merchant revenue. Every budget conversation is a guess.
What we need
End-to-end visibility from campaign source to activated merchant — across every channel, every market.
What success looks like
One number — CAC — that tells us the cost of every acquired and activated merchant, by channel, in real time.
Why Digital Growth needs self-serve segmentation
Today, every audience that Digital Growth needs — for any campaign, any market, any test — must be built by the Data or Engineering team. A single audience request takes more than 5 days — often 1–2 sprints — to come back. By the time it does, the campaign window has often passed. DG cannot experiment, cannot iterate, and cannot scale. Self-serve segmentation changes this: the DG team builds the audience itself — in under an hour, in plain language — without writing code or filing a ticket. That single change unlocks experimentation speed, A/B testing, and the ability to act on data the same day it surfaces.

CAC Payback months to recover spend
CAC ÷ (avg monthly GPV × take rate)
"How fast do we recover acquisition spend?"
Months until cumulative gross margin from a merchant cohort equals what we paid to acquire them. Decision-useful at channel level — tells leadership where to push and pull spend this quarter, not in three years.
CAC Payback < 12 months
The public-market standard for healthy unit economics — gives Martin a number defensible to Finance, leadership, and investors. Methodology (take rate, cost basis, cohort definition) to be locked with Finance — Phase 1 target.
Three independent questions, one programme:
  • Volume — are we growing? Tracked weekly. Directional signal only — growth without efficiency is unsustainable.
  • CAC — are we acquiring efficiently? Daily dial for DG. Tracked per stream: 🟢 Activation CAC (DMO) and 🔵 Lead CAC (BD-assisted). Both measured at Active Merchant so they're comparable.
  • CAC Payback ★ — how fast do we recover spend? The North Star. Quarterly per cohort. Tells leadership when each acquisition channel turns profitable in cash terms — not abstract LTV math, but months to recovery. CAC Payback < 12 months is the standard for healthy unit economics.

The recommendation
A three-component martech stack — working together, not in isolation
① CDP Analytics
The brain
Unifies merchant identity · builds audiences · runs attribution · measures CAC
▸ What does a CDP do?
A Customer Data Platform collects every signal a merchant produces — web visits, app activity, transactions, CRM records — and resolves them into a single merchant profile. It then segments merchants into audiences that DG can act on directly, without filing an engineering ticket. Today this doesn't exist at KPay — every audience is manually built by the Data team, taking more than 5 days per request.
+
② CEP
The activation engine
Lifecycle journeys · email, WhatsApp, push, in-app · triggered by merchant behaviour
▸ What does a CEP do?
A Customer Engagement Platform sends the right message to the right merchant at the right moment — across email, WhatsApp, push, and in-app — triggered automatically by merchant behaviour, not a manual send. HubSpot handles email today but has no behavioural triggers, no push or WhatsApp, and no awareness of where a merchant is in their lifecycle. The CEP replaces and extends this.
+
③ Ad Platform Integration
The targeting layer
ICP-filtered audiences to Meta & Google · suppresses activated merchants · reduces wasted spend
▸ What does Ad Platform Integration do?
Connects the CDP to Meta and Google so that paid campaigns only reach merchants who haven't signed up yet — filtered to match KPay's best-fit merchant profile. Today, activated merchants stay inside prospecting pools and ad spend reaches anyone who fits a broad demographic. This integration cuts wasted spend and improves campaign quality without increasing budget.

  • One screen shows the full programme state — every phase, milestone, deliverable, and open risk in one place. Impact: DG walks into any review with a clear, current view.

  • Risks surface early, not at deadline — slipping items get flagged before they become delays. Impact: DG steers the programme instead of reacting to it.
Cost: ~$2–5K/yr. Candidates: Monday.com or Notion. First spend item in Phase 0. Only tool in the programme that requires zero integration work and ships in days. The cost of not having it is measured in months of invisible programme drift across 4 teams and 4 markets.
